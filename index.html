Statistical Insights
Sum Range Analysis

Historical winning combinations have sums mostly between 100–200. The mean is ~135.

Sum sweet spotCombinations with a sum between 115–175 appear most often in historical draws. Extremes (very low or very high sums) are rare.
Odd/Even balance3 odd + 3 even is the most common pattern (~33% of draws). Pure odd or pure even occurs in only ~5% of draws.
Low/High splitNumbers 1–22 (low) and 23–45 (high) split roughly 3/3 most often. All-low or all-high is statistically uncommon.
Consecutive pairsAt least one pair of consecutive numbers (e.g. 7&8) appears in ~65% of historical draws.
Decades distributionWinning numbers rarely all come from one decade (1–9, 10–19, 20–29, 30–39, 40–45). Spread is typical.

C(45,6) Calculation

Law of Large Numbers
Why past frequency doesn't predict future drawsEach draw is an independent event. The probability of any number being drawn is always 6/45 = 13.33%, regardless of how many times it has appeared before. Hot numbers are not "due" to appear more — cold numbers are not "due" to appear next. The Law of Large Numbers tells us that over millions of draws, all numbers will converge to equal frequency — but individual draws remain random.
About This App

Lotto Analyser Pro applies every legitimate statistical and mathematical tool to Tuesday Lotto (Pick 6 from 45) historical data.

Methods used:

  • Frequency distribution analysis
  • Hot / warm / cold number classification
  • Overdue number estimation
  • Odd/even and low/high pattern analysis
  • Sum range distribution
  • Pair co-occurrence frequency
  • Combinatorics (C(45,6) = 8,145,060)
  • Division probability calculation
  • Balanced set generation

What no tool can do:

Lottery draws are certified random events. No algorithm, AI, or historical analysis can predict a random draw. Every number combination has identical odds. This app is for statistical exploration and entertainment only.
